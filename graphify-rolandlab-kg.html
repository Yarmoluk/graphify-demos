Domain Intelligence — The Numbers Behind Your Work

The knowledge graph doesn't just map what you do — it connects to published research and market data. Here's what the graph pulled from your four clinical domains.

Parkinson's Disease

~1M

patients in the US, projected 1.2M by 2030. 90,000 new diagnoses/year.

Market: $2.28B (2025), growing at 7.16% CAGR to $4.25B by 2034.

Key finding: Rhythmic auditory stimulation at 120 BPM produced the best gait improvement (+16%). Music therapy improves motor function, balance, freezing of gait, and gait speed.

Shawn connection: Your DJ background + multisensory approach directly aligns with the strongest clinical evidence in PD movement therapy.

Sources: Towards Healthcare 2025, PMC4641247, Grand View Research, PubMed 34960415

Multiple Sclerosis

~1M

adults in the US. 50-80% have balance and gait dysfunction. Over 50% fall at least once per year.

Exercise significantly improves balance (TUG and BBS scores). Combination of resistance + aerobic training is most effective.

Key finding: Exercises in different sensory contexts improve dynamic balance — this IS the multisensory approach.

Shawn connection: Your parkour + gymnastics proprioceptive training maps directly to the "varied sensory stimuli" that research says drives neuroplastic adaptation in MS patients.

Sources: Frontiers in Public Health 2024, Journal of Neurology 2025, Physiopedia

Essential Tremor

~7M

affected in the US (only 1.1M diagnosed). Prevalence: 0.8% age 18-30, up to 8.2% age 85+. Massively underdiagnosed.

Non-pharmacological: Neuromuscular physiotherapy, strength training, and functional electrical stimulation show promise.

Key finding: The gap between diagnosed (1.1M) and estimated (7M) represents a massive underserved population. Treatment: medication or brain surgery. Almost nothing in between.

Shawn connection: Your movement-based approach fills the exact gap between "take medication" and "get brain surgery" — the middle ground barely anyone occupies.

Sources: Tremor Journal 2024, JAMA 2024, StatPearls NCBI

Neurorehabilitation Market

$6.18B

projected by 2032 (from $1.95B in 2023). CAGR: 13.68%. One of the fastest-growing healthcare segments.

Movement disorders alone: growing 3X to $2.7B by 2035.

Key finding: MedRhythms' rhythm-based gait system (Movive) just got FDA Class II clearance — validating the exact intersection Shawn operates in.

Shawn connection: You're a human version of what MedRhythms is building as a medical device. The market is validating your methodology with billions of dollars.

Sources: Grand View Research 2024, SNS Insider 2024, Fact.MR, GlobeNewsWire

Parkour + Neuroplasticity

NEW

Emerging research domain. PMC study (2024) shows parkour's open-skill environments enhance executive function, visuospatial ability, and memory through continuous neural reorganization.

Key finding: "Exposure to stimulating and enriched environments facilitates use-dependent plasticity." Researchers call for neuroimaging studies to validate.

Shawn connection: You may be the only certified parkour coach applying these principles to neurological rehabilitation. The research is catching up to what you've been doing.

Sources: PMC12629852, PMC6542405, Physiopedia Neuroplasticity

Rhythm-Based Gait Therapy

120 BPM

Optimal tempo for PD gait improvement. 90 BPM = -8.66% gait speed. 120 BPM = +16.04% improvement. The difference between harm and healing is 30 beats per minute.

Preferred musical tempo: 120-130 BPM. Significant effects on Berg Balance Scale, Timed Up and Go, stride length.

Key finding: FDA just cleared MedRhythms' Movive (rhythm-based neurorehab device) as a Class II medical device, March 2025.

Shawn connection: DJ + movement specialist + neurology = you're sitting on the exact intersection that just became an FDA-recognized treatment category.

Sources: PMC4641247, PubMed 22166406, PMC8582661, MDPI Sensors 2021
